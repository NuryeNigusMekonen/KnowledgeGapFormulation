# Sign-off — DA4

Gap status: closed.

Before the explainer I could see that my Week 11 evaluation artifacts were using different statistical tests, but I could not explain the underlying reason one framing was valid and another was misleading. The explainer made the dependence issue concrete: when the same tasks are evaluated under two conditions, paired methods answer the right question because the unit of comparison is the task-level change, not two independent proportions. What I understand now that I did not before is that this is not just a test-selection technicality. It changes what I am allowed to claim about improvement, especially when the paired sample is tiny and the statistics can swing from a seemingly significant lift to effectively no defensible evidence at all.
